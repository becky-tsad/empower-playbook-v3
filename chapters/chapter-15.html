Case Studies

Four implementations. Different agencies, different populations, same core principles.

Executives Funders 8 min read

Four implementations. Different agencies, different populations, same core principles. Here's what replication looks like in practice.

15.1

Empower Upper Cumberland (Flagship)

Context: 14 rural Tennessee counties. Upper Cumberland Human Resource Agency (UCHRA) serving as backbone organization. $25M TANF Opportunity Act funding. Selected from 83 applicants statewide.

Population: TANF families — 66% already employed at enrollment. Stranded talent, not crisis cases.

Implementation: Full three-team architecture. 17 navigators across 14 counties. Certified employer network in healthcare, manufacturing, skilled trades. SmartDollar financial coaching. Beacon technology platform.

Results

Metric Outcome
Families served 926
Full self-sufficiency (ALICE threshold (225%+ FPL)) 83
Positive exits 145
Annual income gains $7.8M
ROI 7.1:1 (Urban Institute)
Average FPL change 74% → 101%
Employment rate change 66% → 78%
Cliff navigation success rate 84.9%
TennCare savings $744K/year
Childcare slots created 800+
Key Learning

The three-team architecture is what separates this from better-funded programs that produce worse results. Navigators alone produce better case management. Add the Collective Impact Team and Leadership Team, and you get systems change — 800 childcare slots, policy reform, and $744K in TennCare savings that no navigator could have achieved individually.

15.2

UCHRA Agency Adoption (Workforce Expansion)

Context: UCHRA, the backbone agency for Upper Cumberland, integrating Empower elements into existing workforce development and career services work with non-TANF populations.

Population: Workforce development families — broader than TANF-eligible. Many enter at Stage 3 or 4 (already employed, seeking advancement).

What's Being Tested

  • Navigator-led support model applied to workforce development caseloads
  • Benefits cliff mapping for all career services clients, not just TANF
  • Shared data dashboard breaking coordination silos across agency departments

Why it matters: Proves the model transfers beyond its original TANF population when the four Non-Negotiables stay intact. If benefits cliff navigation and navigator coordination improve outcomes for workforce development families the same way they do for TANF families, the model's applicability expands dramatically.

Status: Active implementation. Outcomes data collecting.

15.3

UCDD Agency Adoption (Development District)

Context: Upper Cumberland Development District integrating Empower methodology into regional economic development programming.

Population: Regional workforce populations served through the Development District's existing infrastructure.

What's Being Tested

  • Employer certification emphasis — leveraging the Development District's existing employer relationships
  • Benefits cliff navigation integrated into economic development strategy
  • Coordination between workforce and economic development functions that historically operate in silos

Why it matters: Development Districts exist in every state. If the Empower Method integrates successfully into DD infrastructure, the replication pathway scales through existing government-funded regional organizations — no new agency required.

Status: Active implementation. Outcomes data collecting.

15.4

Recovery Program Integration (Adaptation Model)

Context: Substance abuse recovery programs in the Upper Cumberland region exploring Empower integration for post-stabilization economic mobility.

Population: Individuals in recovery who have achieved initial stabilization (sobriety, housing, mental health) and need a pathway to economic self-sufficiency.

The Adaptation

  • Extended Stage 1 (Getting Started) — stabilization must be confirmed before career planning begins
  • Recovery-specific partners integrated into the wraparound coordination network
  • Navigator relationship serves as the bridge between recovery and career — one consistent person through a period of high vulnerability
  • Benefits cliff navigation is especially critical — disability and recovery benefits have steep cliffs

Why it matters: Without economic mobility after stabilization, relapse rates climb. Recovery programs that end at "sober and housed" leave families one crisis away from cycling back. The Empower Method adds the career pathway that turns stabilization into permanent self-sufficiency.

Status: Framework developed. Pilot partnerships in discussion.

What All Four Cases Prove

The population changes. The agency type changes. The adaptation details change. But when the four Non-Negotiables stay intact — benefits cliff navigation, dedicated navigators, wraparound coordination, and unified data — the model transfers.

That's not a claim. It's an observable pattern across four different implementations with four different populations in one region. The next step is proving it across regions.
