The Phased Approach

You don't launch a systems-change initiative by enrolling families on day one. You build the infrastructure first, prove the model second, and scale what works third.

Executives Program Managers 10 min read

The Empower Method deploys in three phases over 24 months. Rushing phases produces the same fragmented results the model was built to replace.

The Core Question

How do you build a poverty exit system without repeating the mistakes of every other well-meaning program? By phasing the rollout: infrastructure first, proof second, scale third. Every shortcut produces the fragmented outcomes the old system already delivers.

7.1

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 0-6)

Goal: Build the infrastructure. No families enrolled yet.

This is the phase most programs skip — and the reason most programs fail. Enrolling families before your team is trained, your data systems are live, your partners are aligned, and your employers are certified means you're running the same fragmented model you're trying to replace. Just with a new name.

Phase 1 Checklist

  • Create and document shared vision — signed by all partner organizations
  • Hire core team (see Chapter 8): Executive Director, Program Director, Project Coordinator, Lead Navigator
  • Train all staff on Empower methodology, benefits cliff tools, and ALICE/Poverty IQ orientation
  • Map existing programs across your service area — identify every agency touching low-income families
  • Identify and eliminate duplicate services — consolidate or specialize
  • Establish data dashboard — unified intake, tracking, and outcomes in one system
  • Launch employer certification pipeline — begin Poverty IQ training with 3-5 anchor employers
  • Formalize partner agency agreements — shared goals, coordination protocols, data-sharing commitments
  • Set up the three-team architecture (see Chapter 5) — even if roles are combined in smaller implementations

Success Markers — You're Ready for Phase 2 When:

  • Shared vision document is signed by all partners
  • All staff are trained and certified in the methodology
  • Data dashboard is live with test data
  • 3-5 employers are in the certification pipeline
  • Partner coordination meetings are established on a regular cadence
  • Benefits cliff tools are operational and staff can demonstrate them
Common Mistake

Leadership wants to show results to funders and rushes to enroll families before infrastructure is ready. This creates the exact fragmented experience the model is designed to prevent. Funders would rather see a well-built foundation at month 6 than failed placements at month 3.

7.2

Phase 2: Activation (Months 6-12)

Goal: Start serving families and proving the model works.

Phase 2 Checklist

  • Enroll first cohort — start with 25-50 families, not 200
  • Complete comprehensive intake for every family: income, benefits mapping, employment status, childcare, transportation, health coverage, housing
  • Run benefits cliff analysis for each enrolled family — map every threshold
  • Begin financial coaching through SmartDollar or equivalent platform
  • Complete employer certifications — first certified employers operational
  • Establish regular coordination meetings (weekly minimum) across all partner agencies
  • First job placements through certified employers — with employer liaison and navigator support continuing post-placement
  • Collect baseline data for all participants — this is the data your funders and evaluators need
  • Begin tracking all four Non-Negotiable metrics: cliff navigation outcomes, navigator engagement, wraparound coordination activity, dashboard utilization

Success Markers — Phase 2 Is Working When:

  • All enrolled families have complete intake data and personalized benefits cliff maps
  • First cohort of employers is certified and receiving navigator-supported placements
  • Coordination meetings are happening on schedule with partner attendance
  • Dashboard reflects real-time family data, not quarterly reports
  • First families are showing income progression
  • Navigators are maintaining relationship quality — not drowning in caseload volume
Common Mistake

Scaling enrollment too fast. If your navigators are overwhelmed, the model becomes case management with a different name. Manageable caseloads are non-negotiable. Better to serve 20-25 families well than 100 families poorly.

7.3

Phase 3: Momentum (Months 12-24)

Goal: Strengthen what's working, fix what isn't, and build the sustainability case.

Phase 3 Checklist

  • Expand enrollment based on navigator capacity — not funder pressure
  • First families hitting income targets and approaching the ALICE threshold
  • Refine skills training based on employer feedback and regional job market data
  • Document and share success stories (see Rachel Okonkwo's ethical storytelling principles in the agent team)
  • Build funding sustainability plan — begin transitioning from pilot funding to diversified revenue
  • Initiate policy advocacy based on data — what barriers are families hitting that require systems-level or policy-level solutions?
  • First families graduating above the ALICE threshold with confirmed stability
  • Prepare ROI documentation for funders — verified outcomes, not projections

Success Markers — Phase 3 Is Working When:

  • Families are graduating above the ALICE threshold and maintaining stability post-graduation
  • The system is running on established protocols, not daily firefighting
  • You have documented ROI that funders can evaluate
  • Employers are requesting additional certified placements — demand exceeds supply
  • Partner agencies are participating because the model works for them, not because of obligation
  • You're ready to make the case for Phase 4: Scale or Replicate
7.4

Phase 4: Scale or Replicate (Month 24+)

Goal: Expand the model beyond its initial footprint.

This phase is not detailed here because it depends on your Phase 1-3 outcomes. But you should be thinking about it from day one.

The decision at month 24:

Path When It's Right What It Requires
Regional expansion — same organization, more counties Strong outcomes, navigator pipeline, employer demand Leadership capacity beyond current operations, additional funding
Agency adoption — other organizations in your region adopt the method Partner agencies see the model working and want to integrate elements Training infrastructure, shared data systems, coordination protocols
State partnership — state agency contracts for broader rollout Documented ROI, political relationships, government-ready performance benchmarks Policy translation capability (Leadership Team function)
National replication — other states adopt the framework Mature model, proven replicability, technical assistance capacity Partnership with The Poverty Solution

Timeline Reality Check

Phase Duration Common Mistake
Foundation 6 months Rushing to enroll families before infrastructure is ready
Activation 6 months Scaling enrollment faster than navigator capacity allows
Momentum 12 months Cutting navigator relationships short to show graduation numbers
Scale 24+ months Expanding without documenting what makes the model work

The 18-24 month navigator commitment is the backbone. Organizations that try to compress this timeline produce the short-term interventions that Empower's own data shows cause harm. Relationships of less than 6 months produce negative outcomes. There are no shortcuts.

For Funders and Policymakers

The 24-month timeline is not optional. It's evidence-based. Navigator relationships under 6 months produce negative outcomes — they harm families by starting processes that can't be completed. When you invest in an Empower implementation, you're investing in the time it takes to produce permanent results. Shortcuts produce the outcomes the old system already delivers.

Key Takeaway

The Empower Method deploys across four phases over 24+ months: Foundation (build infrastructure), Activation (serve first families), Momentum (strengthen what works), and Scale or Replicate (expand the model). Each phase has specific success markers that must be met before advancing. Rushing phases produces the same fragmented results the model was built to replace.

There are no shortcuts. Build the infrastructure first, prove the model second, and strengthen what works third.
