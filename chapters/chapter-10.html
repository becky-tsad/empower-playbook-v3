Funding Your Implementation

The model works. The question is whether you can pay for it long enough to prove that — and then convert proof into permanent infrastructure.

Executives Funders 10 min read

Most poverty programs die not because they fail, but because they run out of money before they can demonstrate success. The Empower Method requires 18–24 months to graduate families. That means your funding strategy must be in place before your first family enrolls — not when the first grant cycle ends.

The Core Question

How do you build a funding strategy that sustains an 18–24 month intervention long enough to prove outcomes — and then converts those outcomes into permanent infrastructure funding?

10.1

The Funding Mix

No single revenue source sustains a systems-change initiative. The Empower Method requires diversified funding from day one.

Revenue Source Role Examples
Government contracts Anchor funding — largest, most stable TANF, workforce development, TennCare shared savings, WIOA
Foundation grants Strategic investment — fills gaps, funds innovation Truist, Babcock, Blue Meridian, regional foundations
Employer contributions Earned revenue — employers invest because the model reduces their turnover costs Certified employer fees, training partnerships
Community giving Broad base — supplements core funding United Way, individual donors, corporate sponsors
Fee-for-service Future state — as the model matures, technical assistance becomes a revenue stream Replication consulting, training, data platform licensing
The Danger of Single-Source Dependence

EUC launched with $25M in TANF Opportunity Act funding. That anchor enabled the pilot. But anchor funding ends. Organizations that treat a single grant as a business model instead of a launchpad find themselves scrambling at month 18 — exactly when families need them most.

10.2

The Pitch: Lead with Taxpayer Math, Not Sympathy

Different funders need different framings. But the core pitch is the same: this model generates more economic value than it costs.

For Government Decision-Makers

Government thinks in budgets, caseloads, and biennium cycles. Give them numbers they can put in a budget request.

  • Direct cost per family: $2,052 in navigator support
  • Return: 4.4:1 — every $1 invested returns $4.40 in reduced benefit costs, increased tax revenue, and economic activity
  • TennCare savings: $744K annually from families transitioning to employer-sponsored insurance
  • New payroll tax revenue: $183K from increased earnings
  • Caseload reduction: 26 families off SNAP, 20 off TennCare — each one reduces ongoing state expenditure
  • Projected lifetime savings: $24.3M across the cohort — that's the long-term budget impact
The Question for a State Commissioner

"You're currently spending more per family to manage poverty than this model costs to end it. Which line item do you want to defend?"

For Foundation Funders

Foundations think in evidence, systems change, and portfolio positioning. Give them a case they can bring to their board.

  • Evidence tier: Randomized comparison by MEF Associates. ROI analysis by Urban Institute. Control group that converted to the Empower model because the outcomes were undeniable.
  • Systems change, not direct service: Three-team architecture changes policy, employer behavior, childcare infrastructure, and healthcare systems — not just individual family outcomes. 800+ childcare slots. 49 jobs. $744K in TennCare savings.
  • Portfolio fit: This fills a gap. EMPath is urban. CAP Tulsa is urban. There is no comparable rural model with this evidence base. Funding Empower is funding the only proven rural poverty exit system in the country.
The Frame for a Program Officer

"You're not donating to a program. You're investing in economic infrastructure for rural communities that have been excluded from every major poverty reduction innovation of the last decade."

For Employers

Employers think in turnover costs, talent pipelines, and workforce stability. Give them ROI in their language.

  • Turnover cost: $4K–$8K per position replaced. The navigator model keeps employees stable through crises that would otherwise cause them to quit.
  • Wage subsidy: 50% reimbursement for up to 320 hours reduces hiring risk.
  • Talent pipeline: Direct access to job-ready candidates trained for your specific openings — healthcare, manufacturing, skilled trades.
  • The benefits cliff problem you didn't know you had: 1 in 3 businesses report employees turning down promotions. That's not a motivation problem. It's a benefits cliff problem. The Empower model solves it.

If you're applying for less than $4M in total pipeline, you don't have enough opportunities to sustain operations.

The relationship math: Maintain 15–18 simultaneous foundation relationships at various stages — research, cultivation, application, stewardship. This is not a part-time function. Dedicated grant writing capacity is strongly recommended from Phase 1.

Funder Segmentation

Funder Type Lead With Examples
Financial literacy funders SmartDollar outcomes, $407,159 in debt reduction, 89% engagement Financial capability-focused foundations
Workforce funders Employment data, career pathways, employer partnerships, $14.50/hr minimum Truist, JFF-aligned funders
Healthcare funders TennCare savings ($744K), insurance transitions, health system coordination BlueCross, health-focused foundations
Economic development funders Regional ROI, 4.4:1 return, payroll tax revenue, childcare job creation CDFIs, regional development foundations
Systems change funders Three-team architecture, policy advocacy, benefits cliff policy work Babcock, Blue Meridian

What to Avoid

  • Small grants with large applications. Under $50K requiring 20+ hours of proposal work is a losing trade.
  • Mission drift opportunities. A funder offers $200K for a tangentially related initiative. Taking it pulls resources from the core model. Say no.
  • Federal pilots with excessive compliance. The reporting burden can consume more navigator time than the funding supports.
  • Cold applications to invite-only funders. Blue Meridian doesn't accept unsolicited proposals. Build the relationship first; the playbook is how they find you.
10.4

The Sustainability Trajectory

Year Funding Profile Goal
Year 1 80%+ anchor grant, 20% supplemental Prove the model
Year 2 60% anchor, 25% foundation, 15% other Diversify while delivering outcomes
Year 3 40% government contract, 30% foundation, 20% employer/earned, 10% community Convert outcomes to contracts
Year 4+ Diversified — no single source > 40% Sustainable operations independent of any one funder

The ultimate goal: transition from grant-funded pilot to contract-funded infrastructure. When a state agency pays you to deliver outcomes because your data proves it saves them money, you've moved from charity to essential service.

For Funders and Policymakers

926 families served. $7.3M in income gains. 4.4:1 ROI. Independent validation. A control group that switched to the Empower model because the results spoke for themselves.

This model doesn't need belief. It needs investment. The evidence is built. The framework is proven. The only missing variable is whether your community — or your portfolio — includes it.

Key Takeaway

Funding sustainability requires diversification from day one, funder-specific pitch strategies rooted in economic returns, and a disciplined grant pipeline. The 18–24 month intervention timeline means your funding strategy must be in place before your first family enrolls.

The ultimate goal is transition from grant-funded pilot to contract-funded infrastructure — when a state agency pays you to deliver outcomes because your data proves it saves them money, you've moved from charity to essential service.
